Hi, I'm Renata

I'm a recent master's graduate seeking an entry-level environmental consultant or analyst position

Environmental scientist by training.

I have an M.S. in geobiology from Caltech and a B.A. in Earth and planetary sciences from Harvard, which means my coursework focused on environmental science, geochemistry, and microbiology.

I also have four years of research experience in the environmental sciences. Read more about my research.

Quantitative background.

I complemented my science focus with a solid background in math: statistics, multivariable calculus, linear algebra & differential equations, mechanics and E&M for physics majors, and intro computer science. My research is also highly quantitative. I made frequent use of MATLAB and R for data processing, performed regressions and hypothesis tests to characterize relationships, and became an expert at propagating error.

Committed.

I was thrilled to take ownership of each of my research projects and be responsible for their success. Success didn't come easily; every accomplishment followed at least one failed attempt. Long after frustration had overcome my excited anticipation of the results, I was still motivated by a desire to produce high-quality work.

Team player.

Research is a highly collaborative process, so I often found myself brainstorming with, teaching or learning from colleagues. As captain of my high school cross country team, I learned to motivate and build camaraderie among many runners of varying ability. As captain and coxswain of my college intramural crew team, I relished the challenge of judging from the anguished grimaces on my rowers' faces whether to push them harder or let them rest easy.
