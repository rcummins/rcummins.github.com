Climate change in the past

The geologic record of Earth's environmental history holds great potential as a test of the limits of climate change. Recently, a new tool was developed that allows us to directly measure ocean temperatures in the past. I used this tool, carbonate clumped isotopes, to test whether changes in ocean temperature were responsible for a perturbation to the global marine ecosystem 400 million years ago.

Quantitative background.

I complemented my science focus with a solid background in math: statistics, multivariable calculus, linear algebra & differential equations, mechanics and E&M for physics majors, and intro computer science. My research is also highly quantitative. I made frequent use of MATLAB and R for data processing, performed regressions and hypothesis tests to characterize relationships, and became an expert at propagating error.

Research is a highly collaborative process, so I often found myself brainstorming with, teaching or learning from colleagues. As captain and coxswain of my college intramural crew team, I relished the challenge of judging from the anguished grimaces on my rowers' faces whether to push them harder or let them rest easy.
